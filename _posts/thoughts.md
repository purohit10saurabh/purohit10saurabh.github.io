## Depth over action
Some people say that they have a bias towards action. I have a bias towards depth instead of action. For example, I understand on a basic level the math, failure modes, and limitations of a machine learning model before training it, rather than simply training it without understanding how it works. This leads to a deeper understanding. The goal is to understand deeply rather than equating action with progress.

In charlie munger's terms, it is real knowledge over chauffer knowledge. The origin behind "chauffeur knowledge" is this story-
Professor Max Planck gave the same lecture so many times that his chauffeur memorized it word for word. One day, the chauffeur joked that he could give the lecture himself. The professor agreed to let him try. The chauffeur delivered the lecture and the audience couldn't tell the difference. But when someone asked a difficult question, the chauffeur replied, "That's such an easy question that I'll let my chauffeur answer it," and turned to the real professor.    
**Real knowledge**: Understanding the underlying principles, answering unexpected questions, adapting, reasoning from first principles or atleast sustaining your claims with evidence.
**Chauffeur knowledge**: Memorizing jargon, talking points, formulas, or presentations without deep understanding.
Simple questions to test whether you truly understand something:
- Can you explain it in very simple terms?
- Can you apply it in a new situation?
- Can you identify where the idea breaks down?
- Can you answer follow-up questions?

There is a saying `Jack of all trades, master of none is better than a jack of none, master of one.` I probably am top 1% in math and coding. But I am decent in other areas like fitness, reading, travelling, and writing. And I am bad in areas like cooking, singing, playing instruments.

## Types of valuable work
Important but not urgent work is more valuable than urgent but not important work. Urgent work is easily seen by a leader, but important but not urgent work is easily overlooked.

## Jack of all trades, master of none is better than a jack of none, master of one
People are good at different things. To become a jack of many trades, learn from a variety of people good in their respective fields.
