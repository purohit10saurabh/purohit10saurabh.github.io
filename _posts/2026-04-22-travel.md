I have travelled to 15 countries so far. I have travelled solo, with friends, and with strangers on group tours. Of all the places I have visited, these three stand out the most:

- **Rome, Italy**: A mind-blowing city where architecture, history, and food come together beautifully.
- **Osaka, Japan**: Jack of all trades. A well-rounded city that is good(not great) for everything- nature, beauty, culture, nightlife, city life, history, and food.
- **Rishikesh, India**: My favourite place for a longer stay. It has great vibes, great vegan food, fun people, and a deeply relaxing environment.

## What solo travel taught me

In November 2025, I spent a month travelling solo across North India. One thing I realised is that solo travel demands much more from you than travelling with others.

Your decisions carry real consequences. On a trek, for example, you have to carry everything you pack. That means you must think carefully about what you truly need. If you pack unnecessary things, they become a burden. If you forget something essential, you face the cost immediately.

This process taught me a lot about myself. I became more aware of my preferences and limitations, and I learned how to explore independently. I learned to let go of things I don't need. I started going to a new better gym, decorated my house, bought new clothes and made new friends. It is great to see these changes that come from solo travel!
</div>

These gems of learnings and experiences are why I travel. This is a post-hoc explanation based on my experiences. Since everyone experiences travel differently, I think the best approach is to explore different places and different styles of travel, whether solo, with a group of new people, or with friends. In doing so, you find your own gems and express yourself through your own way of travelling.
